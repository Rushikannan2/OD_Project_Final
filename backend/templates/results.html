03
Conclusions
✓
Latent Space Diffusion Works
Operating in compressed latent space significantly reduced GPU memory and training time while generating high-resolution 512×512 OCT images — practical under academic compute constraints.
✓
Conditioning Dramatically Helps
Sobel edge-based conditioning improved SSIM by 82.5% and reduced training loss by 70.9%, confirming that structural guidance is essential for anatomically consistent OCT synthesis.
⚠
GPU Constraints Limit Scale
Experiments were conducted on a 10K subset of the full 100K dataset due to GPU limitations. Results may improve further with full-scale training and higher batch sizes.
→
Data Augmentation Potential
The generated synthetic OCT images have direct applications in data augmentation for downstream retinal disease classifiers, reducing dependency on large annotated datasets.
04
Future Directions
01
Disease-label conditioning: Extend the conditional framework by incorporating disease labels (DME, glaucoma, normal) or segmentation masks for targeted pathology-specific image generation.
02
Faster sampling strategies: Compare DDIM, PLMS, and consistency models to reduce inference steps and enable real-time OCT synthesis for clinical deployment.
03
Attention-based mechanisms: Integrate cross-attention layers and advanced conditioning to improve fine-grained structural details in generated retinal layers.
04
Full 100K dataset training: Utilize the complete dataset to significantly improve model generalization and robustness across different scanner domains.
05
Flow-based generative models: Investigate normalizing flows as an alternative to diffusion for potentially faster and more deterministic OCT image synthesis.
