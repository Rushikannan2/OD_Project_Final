02
Datasets Used

Four OCT scanner datasets were studied for domain shift analysis. The primary training dataset is sourced from Heidelberg Spectralis due to its ultra-high axial resolution and excellent noise suppression characteristics.

Heidelberg Spectralis
Axial Resolution~3.9 µm (ultra-high)
Noise HandlingExcellent suppression
Layer ClarityCrisp & well-defined
Domain ShiftLow (reference)
Topcon Maestro 2
Axial Resolution~6 µm (high)
Noise HandlingModerate noise

Domain ShiftMedium
Topcon Triton
Axial Resolution~8 µm (moderate)
Noise HandlingHigh speckle noise
Layer ClarityWashed textures
Domain ShiftHigh
Zeiss Cirrus
Axial Resolution~5 µm (high)
Noise HandlingModerate-high noise
Layer ClaritySoft boundaries
Domain ShiftMedium-high

The 10K OCT dataset was split patient-wise to prevent data leakage: 70 patients / 8479 images for training, 8 patients / 976 images for validation, and 5 patients / 610 images for testing.
