Project Overview

About This
Research

OCT Synthesis using Diffusion Models — a B.Tech project in AI & Data Science exploring latent diffusion for retinal image generation.

Abstract
This work proposes an efficient OCT image synthesis framework based on Latent Diffusion Models (LDM). A Variational Autoencoder (VAE) is integrated with a diffusion-based generative model to perform image synthesis in a compressed latent space, significantly reducing computational complexity while preserving structural information. Two configurations are explored: unconditional generation and conditional generation, where Sobel edge maps are used as structural guidance.
Model Architecture
LDM · VAE + U-Net · DDIM
01
Problem Statement

Optical Coherence Tomography (OCT) is a non-invasive imaging technique widely used in ophthalmology for high-resolution cross-sectional imaging of retinal structures. It plays a crucial role in early detection of retinal diseases such as macular degeneration, diabetic retinopathy, and glaucoma. However, acquiring large-scale annotated OCT datasets is limited by three key challenges:

🔒
Patient Privacy
Strict patient privacy regulations severely restrict sharing of clinical OCT data across institutions.
💸
Expensive Acquisition
High-resolution OCT scans require specialized equipment and clinical expertise, making large datasets costly.
📉
Limited Annotations
Manual annotation by ophthalmologists is time-consuming, leading to a scarcity of labelled training data.
02
Why Diffusion Models?

Traditional generative approaches like GANs suffer from training instability and mode collapse, producing unrealistic OCT images. Transformers, while powerful, struggle with the fine-grained structural details of retinal layers. Diffusion models provide more stable training, higher sample diversity, and superior image quality — making them the ideal choice for medical image synthesis.

03
Proposed Solution
1
Latent Space Compression via VAE
A Variational Autoencoder compresses 512×512 OCT images into a 16×64×64 latent space, reducing computational cost by ~4× while retaining structural information.
2
Diffusion Process in Latent Space
Gaussian noise is progressively added to latent representations over 1000 timesteps. A U-Net denoising network learns to reverse this process using MSE loss.
3
Sobel Edge Conditioning
In the conditional variant, Sobel edge maps extracted from real OCT images guide the generation process, improving anatomical consistency and layer sharpness.
4
DDIM Sampling at Inference
Denoising Diffusion Implicit Models (~50 steps) efficiently sample high-quality OCT images from the trained model during inference, decoded back to 512×512 pixel space.
